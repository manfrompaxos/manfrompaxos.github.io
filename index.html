BLUF  (Bottom-Line Up-Front)

I spent my entire junior year in High-school studying "Number Theory" and "Algebraic Structures" for my AP tests. I guess the stars had lined up for a few years to have   these  as the course material for the  period. And since the Silicon Valley I once knew has disappeared and the heritage of Andy has been wasted by unworthy successors (BK can you hear me?),  it is time for me to return to my high school hobbies. Little did  I know back then, but all that material  I studied at school every morning and with tutors every evening is the base of cryptography.  This opened up for me the worlds of Quantum Computing and Post-Nakamoto Distributed Systems. I guess having a big name in Fault-Tolerant Computing as a PhD  advisor and being Leslie's neighbor had also  something to do with it.

So if you want to know what is happening on Paxos these days  please watch  Maestro in Blue, because I will now turn  my attention to more counter-intuitive matters from the past. Like every athlete that has to cope with the counter-intuitive aspects of his sport (e.g. skiers must lean into the fall line!!), mathletes interested in establishing trust where no central authority figure is allowed to enter equally must learn to cope with many counter-intuitive ideas.

To that end, I am linking here my hand-written notes from my lectures in the two courses I am teaching this semester: "Design of Quantum Computers" and "Blockchain Engineering".  I am going to upload my QC notes first.
